Home› Shop› Placeholder Haven I

Placeholder Haven I waits at the edge of an evening. The light here has chosen to stay. There is a small door, and behind it a garden, and behind the garden a quieter still — places that ask nothing of you.

This piece began with a single question: what does it look like to make a room for grief that does not insist on being grief? The answer, after many drafts, was light. Soft, warm, persistent light — the kind that finds you before you know you've turned to look for it.

Each component is hand-placed: the moss at the base gathered slowly over months, the windows etched one at a time, the wiring threaded through tiny channels that no one will ever see. The work is not the showing of the work. The work is the quiet evidence that someone cared enough to do it carefully.

This is a place you can return to, whenever the world feels too loud, too fast, too much.
