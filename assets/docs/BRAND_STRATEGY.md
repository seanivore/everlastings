## Executive Vision

**What we're really building:**

Everlastings isn't a miniatures e-commerce site. It's a **grief processing platform** where loss transforms into beauty, memory becomes tangible, and healing happens through community.

The miniatures are the **anchors** - physical objects people can hold and control when everything else feels uncontrollable. But the real product is **permission to grieve differently**, to find your own path through loss, and to know you're not alone.

### The Core Insight

People buy the miniatures because they need:
- **Small spaces they can control** when life is chaos
- **Tangible reminders** that beauty persists
- **Permission** to grieve at their own pace
- **Community** that understands loss without rushing healing
- **A creator** who's living it, not just teaching it

**Turning the intangible into tangible** works in both directions:
1. Abstract grief → Physical miniature (something to hold)
2. Isolated experience → Shared community (something to belong to)

## Revenue Reality Check: Where The Money Actually Comes From

### The Two Paths

**Path A: Volume Strategy (The Burnout Model)**
- Price: $200-400 per miniature
- Needed sales: 5-10 per month for $30K/year
- Reality: Constant production, commodity mindset, race to bottom
- Result: Emy burns out, quality drops, brand becomes "cute crafts"

**Path B: Premium Strategy (The Artist Model)** ✓
- Price: $800-2,000 per miniature
- Needed sales: 2-3 per month for $24K-72K/year
- Reality: Sustainable pace, scarcity creates demand, story justifies price
- Result: Emy maintains quality, content builds value, brand becomes fine art

**Why "trickle sales" is actually the smart play:**

When you sell 2-3 pieces per month instead of 10-15, you can:
- Spend 40+ hours per piece (quality = premium pricing)
- Create content that builds narrative value (story = premium pricing)
- Maintain waitlist psychology (scarcity = premium pricing)
- Avoid burnout (sustainability = longevity = enterprise value)

**This is luxury brand playbook:**
- Hermès doesn't flood the market with Birkin bags → waitlists = desirability
- Supreme does limited drops → scarcity = hype
- Fine artists create slowly → one-of-one = value

**The content strategy justifies the premium:**

Without story: "Why is this $1,500?"  
With story: "Of course it's $1,500 - it's a 40-hour meditation object created by an artist processing her husband's brain injury, featured in a grief healing platform with 5,000 newsletter subscribers."

## The Math Behind Premium Pricing

### Why $800-2,000 Makes Sense

**Production reality:**
- 40-60 hours per miniature (design, sourcing, assembly, lighting, finishing)
- Materials cost: $50-150 per piece
- If pricing at hourly rate: 40 hours × $25/hour = $1,000 (before materials or profit)

**But you're not selling hourly labor - you're selling:**
- One-of-one art pieces (scarcity)
- Meditation objects (emotional value)
- Grief processing tools (therapeutic value)
- Story-infused creations (narrative value)
- Platform membership (community value)

**Comparable pricing in market:**
- Shadow boxes/dioramas on Etsy: $200-800 (mass appeal, less story)
- Fine art miniatures (dollhouse scale): $500-3,000 (collector market)
- Therapeutic art objects: $300-2,000 (emotional value)
- **Grief processing tools with story + community: PRICELESS** (you're inventing the category)

### How Content Justifies Premium

**Without platform:**
- Customer sees: Pretty miniature shadow box
- Thinks: "Is this worth $1,200?"
- Compares: Other Etsy sellers at $300-500
- Hesitates: "Maybe I'll wait for a sale..."

**With platform:**
- Customer has: Read newsletter for 3 months
- Knows: Emy's story about her husband
- Watched: TikTok showing 60-hour build process
- Feels: Connected to grief processing community
- Sees: Waitlist of 2-3 months (social proof)
- Thinks: "This isn't a product, it's an investment in my healing"
- Buys: At $1,500 without hesitation

**The content doesn't just market the product - it CREATES VALUE that didn't exist before.**

### Scarcity Economics

**If Emy makes 30-40 pieces per year:**
- That's it. That's the whole inventory. Forever.
- Each piece appreciates (early works become "vintage")
- Sold-out messaging on website builds urgency
- Waitlist creates FOMO (fear of missing out)
- Secondary market emerges (people reselling Emy's early work at 2-3x)

**This is how artists become BRANDS:**
- Not by making more
- By making better
- And telling the story louder
