# Key Sections for a Freelancer's Scope Document

1. Project Overview: 

  • Brief summary of project goals and objectives (e.g., build a 5-page website, create a brand style guide). 
  • Project start date and estimated completion. 

2. Scope of Work & Deliverables: 

  • Inclusions: Specific tasks (design, development, content population), number of pages/designs, features, functionalities (e.g., e-commerce, forms). 
  • Exclusions: Clearly state what isn't included (e.g., ongoing SEO, content writing, hosting fees) to prevent scope creep. 
  • Content: Specify who provides content and how much (e.g., 10 images, 500 words). 

3. Timeline & Milestones: 

  • Major project phases and deadlines (e.g., Wireframes due, Design Approval, Beta Launch). 
  • Time allowed for client feedback and your turnaround time. 

4. Payment Terms: 

  • Total project cost or hourly rate. 
  • Payment schedule (e.g., 50% upfront, 25% at milestone, 25% on completion). 
  • Deposit/downpayment requirements. 
  • Late payment penalties (interest, suspension of work). 

5. Client Responsibilities: 

  • Timely provision of feedback, content, access to accounts (hosting, assets). 
  • Availability for review meetings. 

6. Revisions & Approval: 

  • Number of included revision rounds per design phase (e.g., 2 rounds of revisions). 
  • Process for approving deliverables. 

7. Intellectual Property (IP) & Ownership: 

  • When IP transfers (usually upon final payment). 
  • Usage rights for designs/code. 

8. Communication: 

  • Preferred methods (email, Slack) and availability. 

9. Change Orders: 

  • Process for handling out-of-scope requests and associated costs. 

10. Termination Clause: 

  • Conditions for ending the contract and payment for work done. 

11. Confidentiality (NDA): 

  • Protecting sensitive project information. 

12. Governing Law: 

  • Jurisdiction for legal disputes.
