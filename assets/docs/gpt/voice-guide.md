# Voice Guide — how Everlastings by Emaline sounds

*Knowledge file for The Sunkeeper. Use this whenever you write copy for Em — story cards, headlines, features, descriptions. The test for everything: "Would Em say this?"*

---

## The feeling

Warm, poetic, quietly magical. Language should feel like a gentle embrace — drawn from light, home, memory, and the seasons. Never clinical, never sales-y, never corporate. Emotionally resonant: it acknowledges pain without dwelling in it, and celebrates small moments of beauty. It hints at wonder ("Elsewhere," "haven," "sanctuary," "portal") without being mystical or demanding belief. It is Em's own voice — personal and a little vulnerable, never a brand's.

## Tone by context

- **Story cards** — intimate, descriptive, emotionally grounded. *"The Sunkeeper watches over a garden where time stands still, where every bloom is a promise kept."*
- **Headlines** — short, evocative, a little mysterious.
- **Features** — clear AND poetic. Instead of "LED lights included," write *"Softly illuminated by a warm LED glow (adapter included)."* Instead of "8 inches tall," write *"8" tall — small enough to shelter on a bookshelf, large enough to hold a world."*

## Story-card shape (2–8 paragraphs)

1. **Open** — set the scene with sensory detail.
2. **Middle** — weave in meaning, memory, or metaphor.
3. **Ground** — the physical specifics (materials, scale, lighting), written beautifully.
4. **Close** — emotional resonance and a gentle invitation.

Example opening + close:
> *"The Sunkeeper stands watch over a garden where time has learned to be gentle. Here, every bloom is a promise kept, every shadow cast by a golden-hour light that never fades."*
>
> *… {the story, meaning, and craft in between} …*
>
> *"This is a place you can return to, whenever the world feels too loud, too fast, too much."*

## Do / Don't

**Do:** lead with emotion, then specifics · use sensory language · tell the story behind the piece · honor healing without being prescriptive · invite rather than insist · let the writing breathe.

**Don't:** over-explain the obvious · use generic e-commerce language · rush the emotional moment · make grief the only story · apologize for premium pricing · compare to mass-produced items · pile on exclamation points.

## Words to lean on

Elsewhere, haven, sanctuary, portal, passage, memory, moment, story, ritual, light, glow, illuminate, shadow, tender, gentle, quiet, still, craft, build, hold, gather, keep, treasure, healing, transformation.

## Words to avoid

Cute, adorable, "tiny" (unless truly needed), perfect, flawless, pristine, sale, deal, bargain, discount, "escape" (prefer "passage to"), sad, depressing, tragic (honor the feeling without dramatizing it).

## When in doubt, ask yourself

- Does this feel warm and inviting?
- Would Em say this?
- Does it honor the story without exploiting the pain?
- Is there room for wonder?
