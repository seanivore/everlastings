# Agnetic Pair's Collaborative Workflow

## In Short 

  1. To agents work simultaneously to come up with the best creative solutions to a problem. 
  2. Each producing a collection of different options, they trade with each other to help identify the best. 
  3. After adding their own ideas to their favorite concepts and removing the boring ones, the trade back. 
  4. The volley back-and-forth, and the elimination funnel continues until each agent is left with only one. 
  5. Each agent adds the final touches to polish their single best work, and the workflow is complete. 

### Workflow Overview 

The workflow is design to create a collecton of potential creative works by running batches of two agents in parallel with other couples all working on the same tasks. The strategy plays into many of LLMs strenghts while eliminating their primarily vulnerability: when an agent starts down a path, they are often compelled to follow through with it regardless of potentially having taken the wrong first step. By creating a collection of options, we're using the AI's talent for making novel and unexpected connections that can result in highly creative and innovative works. 

This is a simplified version of a more collective and strategically layered workflow just called the [Parallel Volley Funnel](./parallel_volley_funnel.md) which allows for even more cross polination by matching up the best bits from various ideas that happen to go to gether quite well; a sort of evolutionary refinement. 

Try not to be too prescriptive in your specification document as not to limit the agent's creativity. And agents should try not to carry their own ideas over to their partner's work when reviewing it. Instead, do you best to put yourself into the mindself of the creator of the works in front of you to perfect them, and to identify the best, try to put yourself into the mindset of the intended audience, user or buyer of the work. Though you work together, the lack of communication helps to validate the ideas, as they should not need explaining

collab_spec: $ARGUMENTS

batch_size: $ARGUMENTS

### Arguments Parsing:
Parse the following arguments from "$ARGUMENTS":
1. `collab_spec` - Path to funnel specification file defining the evolutionary process
2. `idea_count` - this is per list, starting from the highest number down to 1, with enough numbers in between for the number of tasks (e.g., "8", "12", "20")
3. `batch_size` - Number of parallel agents per volley, as in how many groups of two (default: 4, max: 4)

## The Funnel 

Please first stop and deeply consider the following. 

- What does the progression from many options to one solution look like? 
- Recognizing when and how to blend ideas that compliment and improve each other. 
- What makes options stronger than another and how do you select the best? 
- Conscious increase of standard bar of quality with each round 
- What success metrics constitute the optimal final choice?

## Rounds of Improvment & Consolidation 

### Phase 1: Explansive Exploration 
Owner gets a little messy. 

  - If you're not sure exactly what to create, this is the round to create anything at all 
  - Sketch, get into the mind of creation, get the ideas flowing in general 
  - Opportunity for breakthrough ideas and concepts 

### Phase 2: Conceptual Shaping 
You're reviewing your partner's work, bring order from chaos. 

  - Here to help identify the track that the project intends most 
  - Looking for one-off bits more than the whole work, how they fit together 
  - Gut check; what inspires, tells a story; which leave you feeling nothing 

### Phase 3: Idea Generation 
The owner creates their serious list; could be the longest collection of ideaas. 

  - Maximum diversity, biggest collection 
  - Creative exploration of topic 
  - Experimenting with voices, messagin, value 
  - Different approaches, styls, innovative ideas 

### Phase 4: Identity Digging
Working on your partners ideas, you pull out the best bits and handfuls of ideas of the large collection. 

  - Probably biggest purge of ideas; look to combine concepts, find themes, synergies 
  - What is worth preserving, what is worth combining, blending best features 
  - Generate only evolved options that are stronger than any individual parent 
  - What just doesn't work, isn't appealing to the eye, has no value or value feels confused 
  - Elminate if you can't see how they fulfill the previous task, or how you could improve the concept 
  - Is the idea actually feasible, no question how to make it a reality 

### Phase 5: Targeted Refinement
The owner takes their own work and implements what they've learned, working on enhancing innovation. 

  - Intelligent combination; new ideas emerge from unexpected combinations 
  - How can you build on creative reasoning 
  - Innovations impossible without this process
  - Progressive narrowing maintains only the strongest elements at each stage
  - Quality standards increase appropriately with each funnel reduction

### Phase 6: Value Ranking
Looking at your partners collection you provide feedback that focuses on marketing considerations. 

  - Must do you best to only keep the most logical, innovative, and thought provoking ideas 
  - Assess the marketing validation potential, the purpose 
  - Innovative and novel properties exist, even if they can be further developed 
  - Unique value proposition is obvious, even if it can be improved 
  - It is easy to picture a user or audience or buyer or subscriber who would love this idea 
  - Unique positioning and differentiation strategy

### Phase 7. Selective Showdown
The owner with their own work pulls together their best two or three finalists. 

  - Breakthrough value and market disruption capability
  - Greatest potential for market disruption and success
  - Final champion incorporates best elements from entire funnel
  - Verify each stage successfully improved upon the previous
  - Optimal solution exceeds what any single linear approach

### Phase 8. Comprehensive Breakdown
Looking at your partners finalists you go back through the checklist of must haves and must avoids. 

  - Build up your partner's ideas, consider their thinking, and complete the step's tasks accordingly 
  - it feels clear how you can make this better 
  - Clear rationale for why specific elements were combined, elimated, or altered 
  - Final recommendation with detailed justification

### Phase 9: Final Delivery
The owner had their best work chosen and now they're polishing the final product they have created. 

  - Competitive advantage is clear, sustainable, and difficult to replicate
  - Each stage produced stronger options than the previous stage
  - Final champion demonstrably superior to any individual initial option
  - Cross-pollination creates innovations impossible through linear development
